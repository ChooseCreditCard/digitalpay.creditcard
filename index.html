What is a digital payment?

A digital payment is any card transaction made without physical plastic — using tokenized card data, mobile wallets, virtual numbers or stored credentials.

  • Online card payments
  • Wallet transactions (Apple Pay / Google Pay)
  • Stored cards for subscriptions
  • Virtual card numbers
  • Tokenized on-device payments

Tokenization: the core of digital payments

Tokenization replaces your real card number with a secure network token — unique to each device, wallet or merchant.

  • Safer than entering your real card number
  • Merchant-specific card numbers reduce fraud
  • Easy to revoke without replacing the card
  • Strong authentication (biometrics, SCA)
  • Required by Apple Pay & Google Pay
